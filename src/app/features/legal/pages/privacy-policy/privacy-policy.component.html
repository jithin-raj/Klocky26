Klock ("we", "us", "our") provides a human resource management platform used by organisations ("customers") to manage their employees' attendance, leave, tasks, performance and related workforce data. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and the choices you have.

1. Information We Collect

Depending on how you use Klock, we (or the organisation that has invited you) collect:

  • Account information — name, work email, phone number, employee ID, department and role.
  • Attendance & work data — clock-in/out times, location used for geofenced attendance, leave and regularisation requests, tasks and approvals.
  • Device & usage data — IP address, browser/device type, and app usage logs, collected automatically to keep the service secure and reliable.
  • Optional profile data — profile photo, emergency contact details, and documents you or your organisation choose to upload.

2. How We Use Information

  • To provide and operate the core features of Klock (attendance, leave, tasks, payroll-adjacent workflows, approvals, and reporting) on behalf of your organisation.
  • To authenticate users and secure accounts against unauthorised access.
  • To send transactional notifications (e.g. approval status, OTP codes, reminders).
  • To improve reliability and performance, and to detect, investigate and prevent fraud or misuse.
  • To comply with legal obligations, including those under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act) and other applicable data-protection laws.

3. Who Controls Your Data

Your employer (the organisation that has added you to Klock) is the data controller for your employment-related information — they decide what is collected and why. Klock acts as a data processor, storing and processing this information on the organisation's instructions and under contract. Requests to access, correct or delete your personal data should generally be directed to your organisation's HR/admin team first; we support them in fulfilling such requests.

4. Data Sharing & Disclosure

We do not sell personal data. We may share information:

  • Within your organisation, with the managers/HR/admins who need it to do their jobs.
  • With trusted sub-processors (e.g. cloud hosting, email/SMS delivery, analytics) who are bound by confidentiality and data-protection obligations.
  • Where required by law, regulation, legal process, or governmental request.
  • In connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, with continued protection under this policy or an equivalent one.

5. Data Retention

We retain personal data for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide the service, and thereafter for as long as required to meet legal, accounting, or reporting obligations, or as instructed by your organisation. Organisations can request deletion of their data subject to applicable legal retention requirements.

6. Your Rights

Subject to applicable law (including the DPDP Act, 2023 where it applies), you may have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Request erasure of your data, subject to your organisation's record-keeping obligations.
  • Withdraw consent where processing is based on consent, without affecting processing carried out before withdrawal.
  • Raise a grievance with your organisation's designated grievance officer, or with us directly.

7. Security

We use industry-standard technical and organisational measures — including encryption in transit, access controls, and audit logging — to protect personal data against unauthorised access, alteration, disclosure or destruction. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure, but we work to continuously improve our safeguards.

8. Cookies & Similar Technologies

We use essential cookies and similar technologies to keep you signed in and to remember your preferences. We do not use these technologies for third-party advertising.

9. Children's Privacy

Klock is a workplace tool intended for use by employees of our customer organisations and is not directed at children. We do not knowingly collect personal data from individuals under the age of 18.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be notified through the app or by email. Continued use of Klock after a change takes effect constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

11. Contact Us

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy or how your data is handled, please contact your organisation's HR/admin team, or reach us at privacy@klock.app.
